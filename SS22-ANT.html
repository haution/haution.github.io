Description

The main objects of study will be number fields (i.e. finite extensions of the field of rational numbers ℚ) and their rings of integers. We will discuss the notions of integral dependence, Dedekind domains, ideal class group, ramification, étale algebras. We will also cover classical results whose proof involves analytic methods: Hermite-Minkowski Theorem (on the discriminant), Dirichlet theorems (on the finiteness of the ideal class group, and on the structure of the group of units).

Prerequisites

Basic algebra course (determinant, rings, modules). Some familiarity with Galois theory and commutative ring theory (tensor product, localisation,...) will be useful but not necessary, as the required notions will be recalled.
